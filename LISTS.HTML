My Favorite Subject

  • Mathematics
    1. Quadratic Equation
      - A second-order polynomial equation in a single variable x=ax^2+bx+c=0 with a ≠ 0.
    2. Quadratic Inequalities
      - A type of equation which does not have an equal sign and includes the highest degree two.
    3. Quadratic Functions
      - One of the form f(x)=ax^2 + bx + c, where a, b, and c are numbers with a not equal to zero.
    4. Forms of Quadratic Function
      - The general form of quadratic function is f(x)=ax^2 + bx + c, where a, b, and c are real numbers, a ≠ 0, while the standard form f(x) = a(x − h)^2 + k, called the vertex form, where a ≠ 0, and the point (h, k) is called the vertex.
    5. Sum and Product of Roots of Quadratic Equation
      - The sum of the roots is equal to the negation of the coefficient of the second term, divided by the leading coefficient, while the product of the roots is equal to the constant term (the third term), divided by the leading coefficient.
  • Science
    1. Respiratory System
      - It is the organs that are involved in breathing. These include the nose, throat, larynx, trachea, bronchi, and lungs. Also called respiratory tract.
    2. Circulatory System
      - It is the system that contains the heart and the blood vessels and moves blood throughout the body.
    3. DNA
      - The hereditary material in humans and almost all other organisms.
    4. Quantum Numbers
      - The set of numbers used to describe the position and energy of the electron in an atom.
    5. Genetics
      - The study of genes and heredity. Heredity is the passing of genetic information and traits from parents to offspring.
  • Consumer Chemistry
    1. Elements
      - Refers to a simple substance that cannot be broken down into smaller parts or changed into another substance. An element is an atom, which contains protons, neutrons, and electrons.
    2. Compounds
      - A substance made from two or more different elements that have been chemically joined.
    3. Acids
      - An acid is any substance that in water solution tastes sour, changes blue litmus paper to red, reacts with some metals to liberate hydrogen, reacts with bases to form salts, and promotes chemical reactions.
    4. Bases
      - A substance that can accept hydrogen ions in water and can neutralize an acid.
    5. Salts
      - A substance produced by the reaction of an acid with a base. It consists of the positive ion (cation) of a base and the negative ion (anion) of an acid.
  • Edukasyon sa Pagpapakatao
    1. Prinsipyo ng Solidarity
      - Ang pagtataguyod ng kabutihang panlahat kung saan minsan kailangan magsakripisyo ng ilang indibidwal.
    2. Prinsipyo ng Subsidiarity
      - Ang paggalang at pangangalaga sa karapatang pantao ng bawat isa na naaayon sa kabutihang panlahat.
    3. Kabutihang Panlahat
      - Kabuthian para sa bawat isang indibidwal na nasa lipunan at isang pagpapahalagang naiiba sa pansariling kapakanan.
    4. Lipunang Pang-ekonomiya
      - Isang bahagi ng sektor ng lipunan na kung saan kinabibilangan ng ekonomiya o ugnayan sa pagitan ng mga pribado at pampublikong sektor.
    5. Lipunang Sibil
      - Kusang pag-oorganisa ng sarili tungo sa sama-samang pag-tuwang sa isa’t –isa.
  • MAPEH
    1. Medieval Period
      - The period in European history from the collapse of Roman civilization in the 5th century to the period of the Renaissance.
    2. Renaissance Period
      - A fervent period of European cultural, artistic, political and economic “rebirth” following the Middle Ages.
    3. Baroque Period
      - Era in the arts that originated in Italy in the 17th century and flourished elsewhere well into the 18th century.
    4. First Aid
      - Refers to medical attention that is usually administered immediately after the injury occurs and at the location where it occurred.
    5. Weight Management
      - It includes the techniques and physiological processes that contribute to a person's ability to attain and maintain a certain weight.
